Gradient Oscillations Along the Top Eigenvector

The widget below runs the default Chebyshev experiment and tracks how much each gradient step points along the top Hessian eigenvector $\hat{v}$. The eigenvector is locked in the first time sharpness comes within 5% of the critical threshold $2/\eta$. Before that moment the third chart sits empty; once the eigenvector is captured it fills in with two series:

  • $\cos(\nabla\mathcal{L},\,\hat{v})$ (purple, left axis) — cosine similarity between the gradient and $\hat{v}$, ranging $[-1, 1]$. Sign flips here are the oscillation that implicitly reduces sharpness.
  • $\|\nabla\mathcal{L}\|$ (orange, right axis, hidden by default) — raw gradient norm.

Deep Scalar Network — Loss Landscape

The simplest two-layer deep network with scalar weights: $L(x, y) = (1 - xy)^2$. The minimum is the hyperbola $xy = 1$. Drag the white circle to set the starting point, adjust $\eta$ with the slider, and gradient descent reruns immediately.

The loss landscape is colored by $\log_{10} L$: blue near the minimum manifold $xy=1$, red at high loss. The three companion charts show loss, the largest Hessian eigenvalue $\lambda_{\max}$ against the stability threshold $2/\eta$, and the product $xy$ converging toward 1.
